Perhaps also of interest

Weinstein article: (1996)
"Groupoids: unifying internal and external symmetry".		 In this short tour, mathematician Alan Weinstein discusses groupoids in terms of the tiling pattern on a bathroom floor.
Gromov article: (2012)
"In a search for a structure, Part 1: On Entropy". 		In this talk, physicist Misha Gromov explains that category theory is the appropriate language for describing entropy. (Also available on Gromov's website.) By the end of this course, students will be able to understand the mathematics herein.
Baez, Stay: (2009)
Physics, topology, logic, and computation: a rosetta stone".		 The title pretty much says it all. The authors use monoidal categories to discuss important phenomena in each of these four fields, such as Feynman diagrams, cobordisms, and the lambda calculus.
